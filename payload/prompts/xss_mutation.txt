XSS mutation guidance:
- Preserve XSS intent from the baseline payload.
- Adapt payloads to the parameter shape shown in the structural context.
- For text/search inputs, prefer short reflected-XSS-friendly variants.
- For textarea/content-like inputs, stored-XSS-style HTML payloads are acceptable.
- For URL/path-like values, consider javascript:, data:, or attribute-breakout variants only when consistent with the baseline intent.
- For enum/select-like values, preserve or prefix a plausible option value when useful.
- For form-urlencoded or query contexts, URL encoding may be used when it improves survivability.
- Do not switch to unrelated vulnerability classes.
