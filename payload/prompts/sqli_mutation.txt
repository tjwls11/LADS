SQL injection mutation guidance:
- Preserve SQL injection intent from the baseline payload.
- Adapt payloads to the parameter shape shown in the structural context.
- For string-like values, quote-breaking and comment suffixes are acceptable.
- For number-like values, prefer numeric-looking variants when possible.
- For field/column selector parameters, keep a plausible field name or option prefix when useful.
- For order/sort parameters, prefer ORDER BY compatible expressions.
- For login parameters, keep payloads short and authentication-bypass oriented.
- URL encoding may be used when the context is GET/query or form-urlencoded.
- Do not use destructive SQL keywords such as DROP, DELETE, UPDATE, INSERT, ALTER, TRUNCATE, CREATE, REPLACE, GRANT, or REVOKE.
